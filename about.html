About This Archive

This archive exists as a public resource for readers, researchers, and advocates who are concerned about the accelerating collapse of democratic norms and the global rise of authoritarian oligarchy. Authored by Ron Botelho, a Ph.D. student in Complex Sciences at Binghamton University, this collection traces historical patterns and draws systemic parallels to our present-day challenges.

Through detailed analysis of propaganda, state capture, institutional collapse, and the role of disinformation, these essays aim to do more than bear witness—they aim to warn. Inspired by the failures of silence in history, this site is designed to keep the memory of those warning signs visible, searchable, and permanent.

Everything here is freely accessible, non-commercial, and protected under Creative Commons licensing. You are welcome to share, cite, and redistribute the contents with attribution—but not to alter them.

There are no sponsors. There is no marketing. Just the truth—open-sourced, decentralized, and defiant.
