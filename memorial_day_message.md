# A Day of Remembrance, A Call to Readiness

> Good morning, friends.
>
> I won’t say “Happy Memorial Day.”
>
> This is not a day of glee, but of gravity. It is a day weighted with the memory of the many lives lost—so many taken by senseless wars, often fought far from home under questionable causes.
>
> But today, I ask you to shift your gaze—not just backward in remembrance, but forward in vigilance.
>
> We must now prepare for a different kind of war—one not overseas, but here, in our homeland. A war not of bullets and bombs, but of information, institutions, and identity.
>
> What’s at stake is nothing less than the future of our democracy.
