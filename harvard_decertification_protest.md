# They Called Me a Soldier. Now I’m a Scholar. And I See the Danger Clearly.
The Real Danger: What Happens If Trump Gets Away With This.
I’ve seen what happens when people are labeled threats simply for thinking differently. As someone who served in U.S. counterintelligence, I spent years identifying real dangers — not imagined ones invented to silence dissent. The Trump administration’s move to revoke Harvard’s ability to host international students has nothing to do with national security. I’ve interrogated threats. I’ve sat across from people who meant harm. Harvard’s students aren’t enemies. They’re scholars. I know the difference — and this administration doesn’t.”

“After a near-fatal injury, I returned to academic life thanks in part to disability support programs and faculty who believed in my potential. International students deserve that same chance — to be judged by their minds, not their passports. This isn’t enforcement. This is punishment. And I won’t stay silent while the government turns academia into a battlefield. It’s more than a blow to Harvard. It’s a blueprint for authoritarian control through bureaucratic means.

If Trump’s revocation of Harvard’s certification stands unchallenged, we normalize a precedent with three catastrophic consequences:

1. A Warped Immigration System Becomes a Political Weapon
No international student, faculty member, or institution is safe when the visa policy is manipulated to punish dissent. Today it’s Harvard. It could be UC Berkeley, Columbia, or any school criticizing authoritarian power tomorrow. The rule of law no longer protects students, but is subject to the whims of political loyalty.

2. Academic Freedom Is Gutted
Universities must be places where ideas are contested, not controlled. By labeling a liberal campus “unsafe” and revoking access to global talent, the administration is declaring war on free thought. The intellectual independence of higher education — already under assault — could collapse under the pressure of compliance and fear.

3. The U.S. Surrenders Its Global Leadership in Innovation
International students contribute billions to the U.S. economy and fuel scientific, medical, and technological breakthroughs. Purging them to score ideological points is not just cruel — it’s national sabotage. It undermines America’s global competitiveness and damages our reputation as a place where brilliance is welcomed, not exiled.

This decision is not policy. It’s a test balloon for authoritarian overreach. If we fail to resist, we give Trump and his enablers the green light to politicize every federal agency — from Homeland Security to Education — and turn our institutions into instruments of suppression.

We must stop it here. We must stop it now.
Because if academic dissent can be erased with the stroke of a pen, democracy becomes a fiction.
